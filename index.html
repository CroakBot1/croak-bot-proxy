How to Use Bot 1

Step 1: Select a Trading Pair Start by choosing which cryptocurrency pair you want to analyze.
  • Example: ETHUSDT for Ethereum vs. USDT
  • Or: BTCUSDT for Bitcoin vs. USDT
✅ This defines which market the bot will track and score.
Step 2: Choose Your Timeframe Decide how long you want each candle to represent:
  • Scalping → Use short frames (1m, 5m, 15m) for quick trades.
  • Day Trading → Use longer frames (1h, 4h, 1d) for more stable signals.
⚡ Tip: Shorter timeframes = faster signals, but more noise. Longer timeframes = slower, but more reliable.
Step 3: Preload Candles Click the Preload button to load historical candles. This gives the bot enough data to calculate accurate signals and prepare the score system.
Step 4: Start the WebSocket After preloading, click WebSocket to enable live updates. 📊 The bot will begin streaming real-time scores at the top of the page.
Step 5: Interpret the Score Use the score as your trading guide:
  • Below 60 → Weak signal (avoid or be cautious).
  • 60 – 80 → Decent signal (possible entry point).
  • 80 – 99 → Strong signal (higher probability setup).
  • 100 → Excellent / strongest signal.
🚀 The higher the score, the stronger the trading setup.
